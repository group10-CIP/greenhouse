Boston Fern

Boston Fern (Nephrolepis exaltata), also known as the sword fern, is a popular fern species that grows in many tropical areas around the world. It has an evergreen foliage, and sword shaped, blue-green fronds with many tiny leaflets.


Light

Boston Ferns do best in bright, indirect light. Too much shade can result in a slight discoloration while too much light may burn the fronds.


Water

Boston Ferns prefer slightly moist, but not soggy, soil so a moderate amount of water is required. During winter, slightly reduce the watering as the plant is inactive in its growth.


Temperature and Humidity

Boston Ferns prefer mild temperatures, around 65 to 75 degrees Fahrenheit. They can be harmed with temperatures above 95 degrees Fahrenheit or below 35 degrees Fahrenheit. High humidity is also critical for their growth, preferably above 80 percent.


Soil

These ferns like organically rich, loamy soil with good drainage. Poorly drained soil can cause root rot and ultimately kill the plant.


Fertilizer

From the spring to early fall, feed your Boston fern with a liquid houseplant fertilizer at half strength once a month. No fertilization is necessary over the late fall and winter months.
