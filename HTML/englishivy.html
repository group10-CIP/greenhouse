English Ivy

English Ivy(Hedera helix) are evergreen perennials, also classified as wood vines. They can act as ground cover, spreading horizontally reaching 8 inches in height. It is also a climber due to their aerial rootlets, allowing them to climb to heights of 80 feet. The plant is grown primarily for its evergree leaves, but eventually it will start to bloom flowers.


Light

English Ivy has the ability to grow in shade, making it ideal to plant under trees where grasses may not grow well. Indoors though, it may not some more light as it may not be receiving as much sunlight as it needs.


Water

When watering, always check the soil before proceeding. Ivies prefer to be kept slightly on the dry side. The soil must at least be dry to touch on the top before adding water again.


Temperature and Humidity

English Ivy can grow in temperatures between 45 and 80 degrees Fahrenheit. The leaves will stay dark green when grown in consistent temperature and medium to high humidity. It does not like the colds of winter or the heat of summer.


Soil

Soil must be well-drained as the ivy prefers a slightly drier soil to grow in.


Fertilizer

For fertilizer usage, English Ivy must be fed every 2 weeks during the spring and summer season, although do not use if the plant is in a stressful state (very hot, very cold, very dry soil, or leaf production has ceased.)
