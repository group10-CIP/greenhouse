House Plants that Pet Lovers Should Avoid

by: John Christian Tiosan

Indoor plants can be a good addition to our houses. They brighten the mood, provide fresh air and make our place more beautiful. However, a lot of us also have pets in our home who loves to chew on different kinds of things. Unfortunately, some species of house plants can be dangerous to our cats and dogs. That’s why we should be extra careful when deciding which plants we can put in our places. Below is a list of some indoor plants that are poisonous to our pets, which every pet owner should be familiar with.


Aloe Vera

Aloe vera is known for its medicinal use for us humans. It is good in making our skin and hair smoother. Unfortunately, it is not the case for our household pets. The gel substance in its leaves contain an active ingredient called saponin that can cause problems in the pet’s system. Ingestion of aloe vera can cause vomiting, depression, diarrhea, lack of appetite, change in the color of urine.


Jade Plant

Jade plant is a good indoor plant for those people that are not gifted with green thumb. It requires low level of care and can survive in most indoor conditions. However, pet owners should be careful around this. Unlike the other plants on this list, specific toxins it contains are still unknown. Despite this, it still causes some serious problems to cats and dogs when eaten. Some symptoms they may experience includes vomiting, loss of muscle function, slow heart rate and some even experience depression.


Peace Lily

Some members of plants from the lily family are toxic to cats and dogs. Although not really considered a true lily, Spathiphyllum or peace lily is still known to be toxic. It is quite popular as decorations for offices and homes as it is one of the easiest to take care for. It requires low maintenance and can even be grown without direct sunlight. However, if ingested, it can cause mucous membrane irritation, intense burning, and irritation of the mouth, lips, and tongue and difficulty in swallowing to our pets. It is because it contains calcium oxalate, which can also be found on other species of plants such as the poisonous plant dumb cane.


Snake Plant

This one is quite popular in households wherever you go. It is very low maintenance, which requires low amount of water and light that makes it good as an indoor plant. Some even believe that it protects the home from evil influences. It is even sometimes called good luck plants because it can also absorb toxins in the air. On the contrary, it is also one of the plants pet owners should be careful. The entire plant contains saponin toxins. Although considered relatively safe, snake plants can still cause mild effects like swelling and numbness on the tongue if eaten in large doses.


Strelitzia (Bird of Paradise)

Bird of Paradise is one of the popular tropical flowers to add flare to your home. It got its name because its bright orange petals resemble a bird in flight. However, despite its beauty, it can be toxic to our lovely pets. This plant contains hydrogen cyanide or prussic acid, which is an extremely poisonous chemical. In fact, it is even used as a chemical weapon. When swallowed, your pets will experience mild nausea, vomiting, drowsiness, difficulty in breathing, less appetite or worse, death.
