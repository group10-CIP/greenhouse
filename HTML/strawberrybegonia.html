Strawberry Begonia

Strawberry Begonia(Saxifraga stolonifera) are eye-catching plants native in asia. When grown indoors and hanged over a sill or edge, they form a very interesting structure of hairy, red-hued leaves. Strawberry Begonias can be planted and cared for indoors all throughout the year, and they will grow rapidly.


Light

Strawberry Begonia plants prefer bright, but not direct, sunlight for growth. Positioning must be thought of as direct contact with sunlight may burn holes through its leaves.


Water

This plant is a fast grower and prefer ltos of water during its growing season. However, water must be prevented from having contact with the leaves to reduce the risk of fungal diseases. During winter, watering must be reduced but not to the point that the plant dries out quickly.


Temperature and Humidity

Strawberry Begonia prefer a cool environment than hot. Aim to keep its surrounding environment between 50 to 75 degrees Fahrenheit, and a moderate to high humidity.


Soil

Strawberry begonia plants prefer to be housed in lightweight, fast-draining potting soil. It should be moist, but not stay waterlogged.


Fertilizer

Feed weekly during the growing season with a weak liquid fertilizer that includes micronutrients and encourages blooming. Controlled release fertilizer pellets are also excellent.
