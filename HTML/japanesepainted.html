Japanese Painted Fern

Japanese Painted fern(Athyrium niponicum) is one of the easiest plants to grow. It tolerates two conditions that many plants are highly intolerant of, namely, very acidic soil, and deep shade. True to its name, Japanese painted fern is more colorful, offering triangular, silvery leaves that sport an arching habit.

Light

While Japanese painted fern is listed as being a plant for partial shade or full shade, the decision is often made for you based on where you live. At the more southerly end of its range, Japanese painted fern should be grown in full shade. Northerners have more choice in the matter.

Water

A moderate amount of water is required, enough to give the fern a moist, but not close to soggy soil.

Temperature and Humidity

Like other ferns, Japanese Painted Fern requires a mild temperature, a humid environment to grow.

Soil

An essential requirement in growing Japanese painted fern is a soil with excellent drainage. Otherwise, the plant may suffer from root rot and fungal diseases.

Fertilizer

As long as you work organic soil amendments into its soil occasionally, additional fertilization should not be necessary. But if you prefer commercial fertilizers, apply a balanced fertilizer in spring.
