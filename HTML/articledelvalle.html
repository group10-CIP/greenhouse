5 Things You Never Knew About Succulents Plants!

by: Reinelle John Del Valle

It would be an understatement to say that succulents are having a moment right now. But, given how convenient they are to care for, how many colors they come in, and how adorable they are, we believe their popularity is long overdue. Here's what you should know before you go out and buy your next stonecrop or agave plant:


1-It got their name from their thick leaves filled with sap!

The word Succulent comes from the Latin word "Sucus" meaning Juice or Sap. The leaves of succulents hold moisture, which is also why most plants with fleshy leaves also need less water.


2-Succulent plants are resistant to Pest!

Succulents are hardy plants that are mostly resistant to bugs and thrive easily, but that doesn’t mean they’re entirely immune to pests. Typical household insects are not a problem for the most part, but some smaller bugs are attracted to succulents for their large water quantities.


3-People always confused Succulent for cacti!

Cacti are Succulents, but not all Succulents are cacti. Also Cactus plants generally have few or no leaves, while most of succulent plants have big and fleshy leaves


4-Some Succulents Plants are colorful!

Shades of green are a given — but you can also find succulents that are blue, purple, pink, orange, and red!


5-Most Succulent Plants orginate from dry locations!

Succulents first cropped up in areas with long dry seasons (such as Africa), since they store water in their leaves they can easily leave though drought seasons.
