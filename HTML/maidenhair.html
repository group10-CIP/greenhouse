Maidenhair Fern

Maidenhair Fern(Adiantum raddianum) have delicate fan-shaped leaf segments, typically clustered on wiry black stems. Though the are visually stunning throughout all stages of their growth, they are considered a slow-growing fern, typically taking up to three years to reach their full mature size.

Light

Shaded light or partial sunlight are the most optimal light for Maidenhair Fern to grow considering their original habitat is covered by a canopy of tree leaves.

Water

Maidenhair Fern is a thirsty species, so plenty of water is required for its growth. Although too much may lead to making the soil too soggy so a drainage is recommended in its pot to avoid overhydration.

Temperature and Humidity

This fern requires very warm, humid air. Temperature-wise, the fern is best kept above 70 degrees Fahrenheit and should not be placed anywhere in your home where the temperature or cold drafts dip below 60 degrees

Soil

Maidenhair ferns prefer moist but well-draining potting soil. Since it requires plenty of water, a proper draining system is required for the soil not to become too soggy.

Fertilizer

It's not necessary to fertilize a maidenhair fern, as the plant will do just fine without it. However, if you wish to provide it with an added dose of nutrients, feed with a balanced, diluted blend once a month.
