Dixie Wood Fern

The Dixie Wood fern (Dryopteris x australis) is a unique and little-known naturally occurring hybrid fern that is native to the woodlands of the eastern U.S. In their native habitat, Dixie Wood ferns grow on the forest floor in shady, low-light conditions which makes them perfect for shade gardens or those low-light areas of your garden where other plants just wonâ€™t grow.


Light

Dixie wood ferns are accustomed to growing in low-light conditions. A shady area that receives dappled sunlight throughout the day is ideal. However, unlike other fern species, they can tolerate full sunlight as well.


Water

These ferns have medium to high water needs and require consistently moist soil, although they are known to be more tolerant of dry sites than other types of ferns.


Temperature and Humidity

Similar to most ferns, Dixie wood ferns appreciate humid environments although they can tolerate dry conditions if necessary, and they appreciate mid-range temperatures. They do not do well in regions that experience extreme cold or extreme heat.


Soil

Dixie wood ferns prefer moist, acidic soil that is rich in organic matter.


Fertilizer

Dixie wood ferns are considered high feeders and should be fertilized regularly. Fertilize your Dixie wood fern in the late winter to early spring, and again mid-summer with an all-purpose liquid fertilizer.
