Staghorn Fern

Staghorn Ferns(Platycerium bifurcatum) despite being popular today, were once considered plants that are difficult to grow. These ferns are epiphytic, meaning they grow mounted on plaques or other substrates.

Light

Staghorn Ferns prefer to be put in a location with consisten shaded light, however, they can handle more sunlight if given enough humidity, and water.

Water

Staghorn Ferns require frequent watering, although the base should be allowed to dry out about once a week during warmer climate, and once every two to three weeks during cooler climate.

Temperature and Humidity

Staghorn Ferns thrive in warm, humid conditions, though more mature once can survive slightly freezing temperature.

Soil

Though young ferns may be started in a moist traditional potting mixture, staghorn ferns should be mounted once they progress towards maturity.
